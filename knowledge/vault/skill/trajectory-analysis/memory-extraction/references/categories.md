# Entry Categories

## diagnostic-principles

Transferable reasoning patterns -- the "art" of diagnosis. These entries capture how
experienced investigators think about problems, make connections between signals, and
navigate ambiguity.

## signal-interpretation

How to read and cross-reference observability data. Covers what specific metrics,
traces, and log patterns mean in context, and how combining multiple signal types
reveals information that no single signal provides alone.

## anti-patterns

Common reasoning traps that lead investigations astray. These entries describe
failure modes in diagnostic reasoning -- anchoring, confirmation bias, premature
convergence -- and how to recognize and avoid them.

## failure-mechanisms

Recurring fault types and their observable signatures. These entries describe how
specific categories of failures (resource exhaustion, cascading timeouts, connection
pool starvation, etc.) manifest in observability data, helping agents recognize
what they are seeing.

## investigation-strategy

Meta-patterns about how to structure an investigation. These entries cover
investigation sequencing, when to broaden vs narrow scope, how to prioritize
hypotheses, and how to decide when evidence is sufficient.
